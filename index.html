Dr. Hanny El Banna

The man who brought hope to billions

Dr. Hany El Banna in one of his many visits to Africa in company of local school children

Here's a time line of De. El Banna's life:

  • 1950- Born in Egypt
  • 1976- Bachelor of Medicine and Diploma of Islamic Studies at Al Azhar University, Cairo
  • 1977- He started working as a medical doctor at NHS
  • 1981- Dr Hany El Banna was awarded the Hamilton Bailey Prize in Medicine at City hospital (previously known as Dudley Road) in , Birmingham UK.
  • 1983- A pathologist by education, Dr. Hany attended a medical conference in Sudan during a time of famine in the region. The poverty and desperation he witnessed compelled him to return to the UK and set up Islamic Relief as an organisation to help people in need.
  • 1991- Completed a Doctorate of Medicine (MD) in foetal pathology from the University of Birmingham Medical School.
  • 2004- Dr El-Banna was awarded the Order of the British Empire by Queen Elizabeth II. In the same year Dr El Banna also received Ibn Khaldun Award for Excellence in Promoting Understanding between Global Cultures and Faiths within the UK. It was also in 2004 that the Egyptian Medical Syndicate awarded him for his services to humanity and medicine.
  • 2008- Since leaving Islamic Relief, Dr. El-Banna founded the Muslim Charities Forum (MCF), of which he is chairman and The Humanitarian Forum where he is President of the board of trustees.
  • 2010- He was a Founding Partner and Board of Trustees member and President of the Centre for Interfaith Action on Global Poverty (CIFA)
